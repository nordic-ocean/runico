# 🗑️ Trash and Recovery

Deleted something you didn't mean to? Don't panic. In Runico, deleting a folder, topic, or
card **doesn't destroy it** — it goes to the **Trash** first, where you can restore it.

---

## How deleting works

When you delete a **folder**, **topic**, or **card**, it's moved to the trash instead of
being erased. It stays there until *you* decide to remove it for good. Nothing expires or
disappears on its own.

---

## Opening the trash

Click the **🗑️ Trash** button in the top bar. You'll see everything you've deleted, each
tagged with its kind — **Folder**, **Topic**, or **Card**.

(If you haven't deleted anything, you'll just see *"The trash is empty."*)

---

## Restoring something

1. Find the item in the trash.
2. Click **Restore**.
3. Confirm — it returns to its **original location**.

> ⚠️ **One catch with cards:** to restore a *card*, its topic must still exist. If you
> deleted the whole topic too, **restore the topic first**, then the card.

---

## Deleting for good

When you're sure you don't need something:

- **Delete forever** on a single item removes just that one (this *can't* be undone).
- **Empty trash** removes *everything* in the trash at once.

Both ask you to confirm first, because these are the only actions in Runico that truly
can't be reversed.

---

## A worked example

You meant to delete one stale card but deleted the whole *Mitosis* topic by mistake. You:

1. Open the **Trash**.
2. Find *Mitosis* (tagged **Topic**) and click **Restore**.
3. It pops back into *Cell Biology*, right where it was. Crisis averted. 😌
