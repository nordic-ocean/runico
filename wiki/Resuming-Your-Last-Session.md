# ⏯️ Resuming Your Last Session

> The home view remembers your last session and shows how many cards remain.

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/nordic-ocean/runico/main/tutorial/assets/resume.gif" alt="Resuming your last session — looping demo" width="700">
  <br><sub>Looping demo · plays automatically</sub>
</p>

You almost never finish everything in one sitting — and you shouldn't have to. Runico
remembers the practice you were last in and greets you with it the moment you open the
app. Getting back into study is a **single tap**, with no hunting for where you stopped.

---

## How it works

1. **You pause** (or close the app) partway through a deck.
2. **Next time you open Runico**, the home screen shows a big **quick-resume** button at
   the top.
3. **You tap it**, and you're dropped straight back onto the *exact card* you left off on.

---

## The button changes to match where you are

The quick-resume button is smart — it reads the state of your last session and relabels
itself:

| Your situation | The button says | And shows, for example |
|----------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| You paused partway through | **Continue practice** | *Photosynthesis · 3 cards remaining* |
| You finished the whole deck | **Restart practice** | *Photosynthesis* |
| You haven't started this one yet | **Begin practice** | *Membrane transport* |

So a glance tells you both *what* you were studying and *how much* is left.

---

## A worked example

You study **Photosynthesis** during your lunch break and get through 5 of 8 cards, then
tap **Pause**.

That evening you open Runico. The home screen shows:

> **▶ Continue practice** — *Photosynthesis · 3 cards remaining*

One tap and you're back on card 6. No menus, no searching.

---

## Tips

- **You don't have to use it.** You can always ignore the button and
  **[browse](Browsing-and-Practicing)** to a different topic instead.
- **Each topic remembers its own place** too — open any topic in the browser and it shows
  *"Paused at card 2 · 3 of 4 remaining"* with its own resume action.
