# 🗂️ Browsing and Practicing

> Navigate subjects, folders, and topics in a column browser, then practice any topic.

Your whole library lives in a **column browser** that drills down from broad to specific:
**subjects → folders → topics**. Think of it like opening nested folders on your
computer — each time you tap into one, a new column slides in to show what's inside.

---

## The three layers

Runico organizes everything into a simple hierarchy:

- **Subjects / folders** — your broad areas, like *Biology* or *History*. Folders can
  nest as deep as you like (a folder inside a folder inside a folder…).
- **Topics** — the actual decks of cards, living inside the deepest folder. For example,
  *Photosynthesis* or *Membrane transport*.
- **Cards** — the individual flashcards inside a topic.

> 📚 Not sure what "topic" vs "folder" means? The **[Glossary](Glossary)** has plain-English
> definitions.

---

## How to navigate

1. **Start at _Everything_** — the top column shows your whole library and what's due today.
2. **Tap a folder** — a new column slides in showing what's inside it.
3. **Keep drilling** until you reach a **topic**.
4. **Tap the topic** — it opens an **action card** (see below).

On a phone, the columns scroll sideways and the one you just opened slides to the center.

---

## Practice the whole column with "Practice all"

Every column has a **Practice all** button. It studies *everything* in that column at
once — so you can practice a single topic, or a whole folder full of topics, in one
session. Handy when you want a broad review.

---

## The action card (what a topic opens to)

When you tap a topic, it opens an **action card** — a little summary panel with everything
you can do:

- A small **accuracy-trend sparkline** at the top — a glance at how recent sessions are going.
- **Stats** — e.g. *22 cards · 4 due · last 9d ago*.
- **A resume hint** — e.g. *Paused at card 2 · 3 of 4 remaining*.
- **Actions:**
  - **Continue / Begin practice** → start studying.
  - **Open cards** → see and edit the individual cards.
  - **[View progress](Tracking-Your-Progress)** → the full accuracy chart over time.
  - **[Add more AI Cards](Adding-Cards)** → grow the topic with new cards.

---

## A worked example

You want to review cells. You:

1. Open **Biology** → a column shows *Cell Biology, Genetics, Ecology*.
2. Open **Cell Biology** → a column shows *Organelles, Membrane transport, Mitosis,
   Photosynthesis*.
3. Tap **Photosynthesis** → its action card opens.
4. Tap **Continue practice** → you're studying. ✨

Or, if you'd rather review *all* of Cell Biology at once, tap **Practice all** in the Cell
Biology column instead.
