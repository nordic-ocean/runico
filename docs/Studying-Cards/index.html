📖 Studying Cards

Recall the answer, reveal it, then Continue or Pause — no grading scales to fuss over.


Looping demo · plays automatically

Studying in Runico is deliberately simple. A card shows you a prompt with the key idea hidden. You try to recall it from memory, reveal the answer to check yourself, and then make one of two choices. There is no "how well did you remember it, 1 to 5?" scale to agonize over.

How a study session works

  1. A card appears with the key idea blanked out. Try to recall it before you reveal — that little moment of effort is what makes flashcards work.
  2. Tap Show answer to uncover the hidden part and check yourself.
  3. Now you have two buttons: - Continue → you're done with this card; move to the next one. - Pause → stop the session. Runico saves the exact card you're on so you can come back later.
  4. Repeat until the deck is finished (or you pause).

That's the whole loop. 🎉

A worked example

Say you're studying cell biology. A card shows:

The [      ] contains the cell's genetic material.

You think: "nucleus." You tap Show answer:

The nucleus contains the cell's genetic material.

You got it right, so you tap Continue. On to the next card.

What you'll see on screen

  • The prompt, with the answer blanked out (this style of card is called a cloze — see the Glossary).
  • A progress marker like 1 of 4, so you always know how far along you are.
  • Before revealing: a big Show answer button (and a quiet Pause).
  • After revealing: the Pause and Continue buttons.
  • A footer with a Source link — tap it to read the original material this card came from.

Two kinds of cards you'll study

  • Text cards — a prompt with the answer hidden, exactly like the example above.
  • Image-occlusion cards — a picture with a label box covered up. Instead of "Show answer," you reveal what's under the box. Great for diagrams and anatomy. See Image-Occlusion Cards.

Keyboard shortcuts (desktop)

On a computer with a keyboard, you can fly through a session without the mouse:

Key Does
Space Show the answer
Enter Continue (got it)
X Mark missed
Esc Pause

📱 On phones and tablets these shortcuts don't show up — you just tap the on-screen buttons instead.

Tips for beginners

  • Be honest with yourself. Reveal after you've made a real guess, not before.
  • Short and often beats long and rare. A few minutes a day is plenty.
  • Don't worry about "missing" a card. It just means you'll see it again — that's the point.
