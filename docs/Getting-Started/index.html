Getting Started

This page takes you from never having seen Runico to your first study session. No prior experience needed — if you can use a web browser, you can use Runico.

Step 1 — Open Runico

You have two choices. For most people, the browser is the easiest start.

Option A: In your web browser (recommended)

Just click here: ▶ Open Runico

Nothing to install, no account to make. It works in Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox, and most modern browsers.

💡 Tip: Chrome or Edge give you the smoothest experience because Runico can auto-save your work straight to a file. Other browsers work too — they just save by downloading the file. See Your Library File for the details.

Option B: Download the desktop app

Prefer a real app on your computer? Grab an installer for macOS, Windows, or Linux from the Releases page.

⚠️ The desktop apps are unsigned, so on first launch your system may warn about an "unidentified developer." That's expected. On macOS, right-click the app → Open; on Windows, click More info → Run anyway.

Step 2 — Create your library

The first thing you'll see is the Start screen, asking you to open a library or create a new one.

A library is simply the file that holds everything you make in Runico — all your folders, topics, cards, and study history, in one place.

  • First time? Click New to create a fresh, empty library.
  • Returning, or moving from another device? Click Open and pick your existing .runico file.

📁 Want to understand exactly what this file is, where it lives, and how to back it up? Read Your Library File.

Step 3 — Put some cards in it

A brand-new library is empty, so let's add something to study.

The fastest way is to let Runico make cards for you: drop in a page of notes, a screenshot, or a textbook figure, and it drafts flashcards you can review and keep.

👉 Full walkthrough: Adding Cards

(You can also write cards by hand, or paste cards you generated in your own AI chat like ChatGPT or Claude — both are covered on that page.)

Step 4 — Study

Open a topic and start practicing. For each card you:

  1. Recall the answer in your head.
  2. Reveal it to check yourself.
  3. Tap Continue to move on, or Pause to stop and save your place.

That's the entire study loop — no "rate yourself 1–5" scales.

👉 Full walkthrough: Studying Cards

Step 5 — Come back later

Whenever you return, the home screen greets you with a Continue practice button that drops you right back onto the card you stopped on. Nothing to hunt for.

👉 See Resuming Your Last Session.
