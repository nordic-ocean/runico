🔎 Seeing the Source

Open the original material as a book page, with the studied term highlighted in context.


Looping demo · plays automatically

A flashcard on its own can feel like a fact floating in space. Runico fixes that: every card remembers where it came from. One tap opens the original material — the page, figure, or notes the card was made from — with the term you're studying highlighted right where it appears. It's context, not just a flashcard.

How to see a card's source

  1. While studying (or reviewing), look at the card footer.
  2. Tap the Source link (it's labelled with where the card came from, e.g. Cell Biology · Ch. 4).
  3. The source opens like a book page — flowing prose around any figure — with your studied term highlighted inline.

What it looks like

  • A heading like "From this source · Eukaryotic cell · Ch. 4."
  • Readable, textbook-style prose, with the studied term (e.g. nucleus) highlighted in place so your eye goes straight to it.
  • Any embedded figure, with its caption (e.g. Figure 4.3 — A generalized eukaryotic cell. Highlighted: Nucleus).

Why it's useful

  • Understand, don't just memorize. Seeing the surrounding sentence helps the fact stick because it's connected to meaning.
  • Settle a doubt instantly. Not sure why an answer is what it is? The source is one tap away — no leaving the app to dig through your notes.
  • Trust your cards. You can always check a card against the material it was drafted from.

A worked example

You're studying a card about the nucleus and think "wait, is that the same as the nucleolus?" You tap Source, the original page opens with nucleus highlighted in a full sentence, and the distinction is clear in two seconds. You tap back and keep going.
