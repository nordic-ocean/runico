# 5 · Add cards from anything

> Drop in material, let Runico draft cards, then keep the ones worth studying.

<p align="center">
  <img src="assets/add.gif" alt="Add cards from anything — looping demo" width="700">
  <br><sub>Looping demo · plays automatically</sub>
</p>

Turn any material into cards — a textbook page, a screenshot, your notes. Drop in a source,
name the topic, and Runico reads it and drafts cards for you. You review each draft and
keep only the ones worth studying.

## Walkthrough

1. **Turn any material into cards.** A textbook page, a screenshot, your notes.
2. **Drop in your source — here, a textbook figure.**
3. **Name the topic, then _Begin_.**
4. **Runico reads it and drafts cards for you.** (_Reading → Understanding → Drafting cards → Choosing the best_)
5. **Review each draft — _Keep_ or _Remove_.**
6. **Keep the good ones.**
7. **When you're done, they land in the topic.**

## What you see

- A destination breadcrumb — **New AI Cards in** _Biology › Cell Biology_.
- A **"What are you learning?"** prompt with a topic name (e.g. _Cell signaling_).
- A drop zone: **"Drop, paste, or browse"** — _an image, a chapter, a screenshot, a page of notes._
  Once a file lands it shows a thumbnail, size, and a ready check (e.g. _cell-biology-ch4.png · 1.2 MB · ready to read_).
- A background processing screen with a progress bar that cycles through
  _Reading. → Understanding. → Drafting cards. → Choosing the best._
- A review screen — **Card 1 of 6 · Drafted from source** — with **Remove**, **Edit**, and **Keep** actions.
- A confirmation — **5 cards added to _Cell signaling_** — with **View cards** and **Back home**.
