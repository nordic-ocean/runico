# 7 · See the source

> Open the original material as a book page, with the studied term highlighted in context.

<p align="center">
  <img src="assets/source.gif" alt="See the source — looping demo" width="700">
  <br><sub>Looping demo · plays automatically</sub>
</p>

Every card remembers where it came from. Tap **Source** and Runico opens the original
material as a book page — prose around the figure — with the term you're studying
highlighted in place. It's context, not just a flashcard.

## Walkthrough

1. **Every card remembers where it came from.**
2. **Tap _Source_ to open the original.**
3. **It reads like a book page — prose around the figure.** Context, not just a flashcard.
4. **The term you're studying is highlighted in place.**

## What you see

- A **Source** button on the card footer (e.g. _Cell Biology · Ch. 4_).
- A source page titled **"From this source · Eukaryotic cell · Ch. 4"** that reads like a textbook:
  flowing prose with the studied term (e.g. **nucleus**) highlighted inline.
- An embedded figure with a caption (_Figure 4.3 — A generalized eukaryotic cell. Highlighted: Nucleus._).
